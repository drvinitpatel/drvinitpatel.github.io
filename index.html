Educational Journey

My academic journey has shaped my understanding of psychiatry and addiction medicine. I've had the privilege of training at some of India's leading medical institutions, which provided me with a strong clinical foundation and a deep appreciation for research-driven practice.

My DM in Addiction Psychiatry at AIIMS, New Delhi allowed me to refine my expertise in managing complex substance use disorders. The program deepened my understanding of the neurobiology of addiction, public health implications, and evidence-based interventions for recovery and harm reduction. It was an immersive experience that combined research, clinical practice, and policy-level engagement.

Research & Extramural Projects

Focused on addiction, psychiatric comorbidities, and evidence-based interventions.

Exploring addiction, behavioral patterns, and psychiatric comorbidities.

Understanding the Mind–Substance Interface

Opioid Use Disorders

Clinical, biological, and systems research on opioid dependence. This includes studies on opioid agonist treatment (OAT), comparative effectiveness of buprenorphine and methadone, determinants of treatment retention and adherence, and biological investigations examining sexual health, hormonal correlates, and related clinical outcomes among individuals with opioid use disorders.

Health Systems and Policy Research

Critical analysis of how laws, policies, and governance structures influence addiction and mental health care delivery. This includes examining the NDPS Act, the Mental Healthcare Act (MHCA), and national drug demand reduction strategies, with an emphasis on ethical practice, patient rights, and health system reform.

Digital Interventions in Mental Health and Addiction

Design and evaluation of technology-enabled interventions, including mobile adherence systems, telepsychiatry platforms, and AI-assisted tools, to enhance accessibility, engagement, and continuity of care.
